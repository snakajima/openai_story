**Runtime**: 60 minutes

## LOGLINE

Over the crisis weekend of November 18-20, 2023, Ilya watches horrified as his board discusses merging OpenAI with rival Anthropic, then board member Helen Toner shockingly states that destroying the company would be "consistent with the mission"—pushing Ilya to his breaking point and a public tweet of regret.

---

## ONE-PARAGRAPH SUMMARY

The crisis weekend continues. Saturday evening: A video call with Anthropic's founders Dario and Daniela Amodei to discuss merging and having Anthropic take over OpenAI's leadership. Ilya is "very unhappy"—he really didn't want to merge with Anthropic—but Helen is "most supportive." The proposal dies quickly due to "practical obstacles," but worse is coming. Sunday: In a meeting with executives, they warn that without Sam's return OpenAI will be destroyed. Helen calmly responds that destruction would be "consistent with the mission"—and according to Ilya, she said it "even more directly" than that. Ilya's reaction: "definitely No for me." This is extremism he can't support. That night, alone and broken, Ilya types a tweet: "I deeply regret my participation in the board's actions." He hesitates, then hits send. The reversal is public and irreversible.

## KEY SCENES

1. **COLD OPEN - Anthropic Call Begins** (4 min)
   - Saturday evening, continuing from Ep 4 cliffhanger
   - Video call: Board + Anthropic representatives
   - Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, at least one other visible
   - Dario: "We're excited about the possibility. Anthropic leadership would run the combined entity."
   - Daniela: "Of course, there are practical obstacles..."
   - Helen: Engaged, supportive
   - Ilya: Off-camera, seething, jaw clenched

2. **DEPOSITION INTERLUDE 1 - The Anthropic Testimony** (4 min)
   - Q: "Tell me about the Anthropic merger proposal."
   - A: "I don't know whether it was Helen who reached out or whether Anthropic reached out to Helen. But they reached out with a proposal to be merged with OpenAI and take over its leadership."
   - Q: "What was your response?"
   - A: "I was very unhappy about it."
   - Q: "Why?"
   - A: "Because I really did not want OpenAI to merge with Anthropic."
   - Q: "Why not?"
   - A: "I just didn't want to."
   - Simple, emotional, honest
   - Q: "What about the other board members?"
   - A: "They were a lot more supportive, yes."
   - Q: "Who was most supportive?"
   - A: "My recollection is that Helen was the most supportive."

3. **Board Discussion Post-Call** (12 min)
   - After Anthropic call ends
   - Helen: "This could solve our problem. Anthropic's leadership is mission-aligned."
   - Adam: "What about OpenAI's independence?"
   - Helen: "What independence? We're about to lose everyone to Microsoft."
   - Ilya: "No. This is wrong. We can't just hand OpenAI to a competitor."
   - Helen: "They're not a competitor in values. They share our mission."
   - Ilya: "I really don't want to merge with Anthropic."
   - Helen: "What do you want then? Everyone quits, we have nothing."
   - Ilya: Isolated, realizing lost control of his own coup
   - Adam mediating
   - Tasha quiet
   - The merger dies ("practical obstacles" from Anthropic)
   - But damage done: Board willing to destroy OpenAI's independence

4. **Sunday Morning - Pressure Mounting** (8 min)
   - **Title card**: "NOVEMBER 19, 2023 - SUNDAY"
   - Employee letter signatures still climbing
   - Media coverage intensifying
   - Calls from lawyers, advisors
   - Options narrowing
   - Board members exhausted, isolated
   - Inevitability building: "We have to bring Sam back"

5. **The Crisis Meeting - Setup** (5 min)
   - Meeting arranged: Board members + Executive team
   - Executives will present their case
   - Board must respond
   - Tension thick
   - This could be the deciding moment

6. **The Destruction Statement** (15 min)

   **Executives Present Their Case**:
   - "If Sam doesn't return, OpenAI will be destroyed"
   - "700 employees will leave"
   - "Microsoft will take them all"
   - "The company will cease to exist"
   - "This is inconsistent with OpenAI's mission"
   - Clear, urgent, pleading

   **Silence**

   **Helen Speaks**:
   - Calm, matter-of-fact
   - "Actually, that would be consistent with the mission."
   - **SHOCKED SILENCE**
   - Jaws dropping
   - Executives stunned
   - Even Adam and Tasha uncomfortable
   - Ilya's face: Horror

7. **DEPOSITION INTERLUDE 2 - Helen's Statement** (5 min)
   - Q: "Do you recall Helen saying that?"
   - A: "I do recall."
   - Q: "What was the context?"
   - A: (Ilya explains the meeting)
   - Q: "And she said destroying OpenAI would be consistent with the mission?"
   - A: "Something to the effect of that it is consistent, but I think she said it even more directly than that."
   - Q: "More directly?"
   - A: "Yes."
   - Implication: Even harsher than testified
   - Q: "What was your reaction?"
   - A: "I could imagine hypothetical extreme circumstances that answer would be 'Yes'; but at that point in time, the answer was definitely 'No' for me."
   - **"Definitely No for me"** - Ilya's rejection of extremism

8. **Ilya After the Meeting** (6 min)
   - Alone, processing
   - Realization: Aligned with someone willing to destroy OpenAI
   - This isn't what he wanted
   - Wanted to save OpenAI, not destroy it
   - Helen has taken mission purity too far
   - Everything is wrong
   - How did it come to this?

9. **The Pressure Continues** (5 min)
   - Sunday night into Monday
   - Calls, messages, pressure from all sides
   - "Board has to resign"
   - Media firestorm
   - No good options left
   - Inevitability settling in
   - Sam will return
   - Board will fall

10. **Ilya's Breaking - The Tweet** (15 min)

    **Ilya Alone** (Home/Office):
    - Late Sunday/Early Monday
    - Can't sleep
    - Everything he tried to do backfired
    - Tried to protect OpenAI → Nearly killed it
    - Tried to act on mission → Aligned with extremist
    - Trusted Mira completely → Never verified
    - Expected employees neutral → They revolted
    - Everything based on assumptions, secondhand knowledge
    - All wrong

    **Opening Twitter**:
    - Types: "I deeply regret my participation in the board's actions. I never intended to harm OpenAI. I love everything we've built together and I will do everything I can to reunite the company."
    - Stares at the words
    - This is public
    - This is final
    - This admits it was wrong
    - Finger hovering over "Tweet" button

    **The Decision**:
    - Deep breath
    - Thinks of Helen's statement: Destruction "consistent"
    - Thinks of employees: 700+ demanding change
    - Thinks of Sam: Maybe he was wrong about everything
    - Thinks of Mira: Never verified
    - **Clicks "Tweet"**
    - It posts
    - Live, spreading
    - Irreversible
    - The reversal is public

    **FREEZE FRAME** on the tweet appearing on screen
