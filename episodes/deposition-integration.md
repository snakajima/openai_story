# Deposition Integration Plan

**Series**: The Board Room Coup
**Document Type**: Technical Planning
**Purpose**: Comprehensive strategy for integrating October 1, 2025 deposition testimony as narrative framing device

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The deposition serves as the primary framing device for "The Board Room Coup," anchoring the dramatized events of 2023-2024 in the present-day (2025) legal context of Musk v. Altman. This document outlines the complete strategy for integrating 82 minutes of deposition footage across 6 episodes, creating a documentary-drama hybrid that reveals the story through Ilya Sutskever's sworn testimony.

**Total Deposition Time**: 82 minutes across 340-minute series (~24% of runtime)
**Format**: Videotaped deposition, attorneys' eyes only
**Location**: Cooley LLP, San Francisco
**Date**: October 1, 2025
**Time**: 10:19 AM - Evening (10+ hours compressed)
**Witness**: Ilya Sutskever (former Chief Scientist, OpenAI)

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Deposition as Dramatic Revelation
- Not mere narration - active revelation of shocking truths
- Q&A format creates inherent dramatic tension
- Ilya's hesitations, pauses, word choices matter
- Present-day Ilya knows what past Ilya didn't

### 2. Documentary Authenticity
- Visual style: Flat institutional lighting, video quality
- Camera: Fixed angle on witness (occasionally questioner reverse)
- Sound: Room tone, chair movements, paper shuffling
- Timestamps visible: "October 1, 2025 10:19 AM" etc.
- Text overlays: "Q:" and "A:" labels

### 3. Strategic Placement
- Deposition interludes at moment of maximum dramatic irony
- Reveals information audience needs when they need it
- Creates contrast between past certainty and present regret
- Builds throughout series to climactic Episode 6 dominance

### 4. Seamless Transitions
- Question triggers flashback to event being discussed
- Never jarring - always motivated by testimony content
- Return transitions often on significant admissions
- Visual and audio bridges carefully designed

### EPISODE 5: "The Collapse" (9 minutes deposition)

**Function**: Detail Anthropic proposal, Helen's statement, setup for breaking

**Segment 1: THE ANTHROPIC TESTIMONY** (4 minutes)

- **Visual**: Resume deposition, evening
- **Content**:
  - Q: "Tell me about the Anthropic merger proposal."
  - A: "I don't know whether it was Helen who reached out or whether Anthropic reached out to Helen. But they reached out with a proposal to be merged with OpenAI and take over its leadership."
  - Q: "What was your response?"
  - A: "I was very unhappy about it."
  - Q: "Why?"
  - A: "Because I really did not want OpenAI to merge with Anthropic."
  - Q: "Why not?"
  - A: "I just didn't want to."
  - **Simple, emotional honesty**
  - Q: "What about the other board members?"
  - A: "They were a lot more supportive, yes."
  - Q: "Who was most supportive?"
  - A: "My recollection is that Helen was the most supportive."

**Segment 2: HELEN'S STATEMENT** (5 minutes)
- **Timestamp**: October 1, 2025, 7:23 PM
- **Visual**: Close-up on Ilya for this critical testimony
- **Content**:
  - Q: "Do you recall Helen saying that destroying the company would be consistent with the mission?"
  - A: "I do recall."
  - Q: "What was the context?"

## TRANSITION TECHNIQUES

### Deposition → Flashback

**Technique 1: Question as Trigger**
- Lawyer asks about specific event
- Hold on Ilya's face as he remembers
- Visual: His eyes shift (looking into past)
- Audio: Voice begins answer, fades under
- Image: Dissolve or cut to past event
- **Example**: "Tell me about the first time Mira approached you" → Flashback to conference room

**Technique 2: Verbal Bridge**
- Ilya begins describing event in deposition
- His voice continues over flashback imagery
- Voice fades as flashback action takes over
- **Example**: "I received a message from Mira..." (voice over flashback of phone screen)

**Technique 3: Visual Echo**
- Deposition shows document/exhibit
- Flashback begins with same image in context
- **Example**: Screenshot shown in deposition → Flashback to Mira showing that screenshot to Ilya

**Technique 4: Timestamp Transition**
- Deposition timestamp visible: "October 1, 2025, 2:45 PM"
- Timestamp morphs: "Spring 2023"
- Scene transitions to past
- **Example**: Date change visible on screen

### Flashback → Deposition

**Technique 1: Dramatic Irony Peak**
- Flashback shows Ilya confident/certain
- Cut to present-day Ilya's regretful face
- Contrast emphasized
- **Example**: Past Ilya "I'm certain" → Present Ilya "I was wrong"

**Technique 2: Moment of Impact**
- Flashback shows shocking event
- Cut immediately to present-day testimony about it
- "What happened next?"
- **Example**: Employee revolt begins → Cut to "Immediate revolt" testimony

**Technique 3: Emotional Punch**
- Flashback builds to emotional climax
- Cut to present-day face showing those emotions remembered
- **Example**: Ilya's breaking point → Present Ilya's face showing he still feels it

**Technique 4: Question Interruption**
- Flashback scene playing
- Lawyer's voice breaks in: "And then what happened?"
- Snap back to deposition
- **Example**: Mid-scene, voice interrupts, back to present

## VISUAL DIFFERENTIATION

### Deposition Look (2025)
- **Color**: Slightly desaturated, neutral
- **Lighting**: Flat, institutional, fluorescent
- **Camera**: Fixed angles, video quality (not film quality)
- **Framing**: Centered, composed, static
- **Environment**: Conference room, generic
- **Indicators**: Timestamp, "Attorneys' Eyes Only" watermark (subtle)

- **Color**: Full saturation, warmer or cooler by mood
- **Lighting**: Cinematic, motivated, varied
- **Camera**: Mobile, film quality, dynamic
- **Framing**: Varied, expressive, moving
- **Environment**: Specific locations, detailed
- **Indicators**: Title cards for dates ("November 18, 2023")

### Visual Cues for Audience
- Deposition always feels "video"
- Flashbacks always feel "film"
- Immediate recognition of which timeframe
- No confusion between present and past

## THEMATIC INTEGRATION

### Firsthand vs Secondhand Knowledge
- **Flashbacks**: Show secondhand information chain
- **Deposition**: Reveals he never got firsthand knowledge
- **Contrast**: What he thought vs what was true
- **Thesis**: "Critical importance of firsthand knowledge" (Ep 6)

### Certainty vs Doubt
- **Flashbacks**: Past Ilya certain, confident
- **Deposition**: Present Ilya knows he was wrong
- **Contrast**: Certainty without verification is dangerous
- **Arc**: Confident (Ep 1-3) → Failing (Ep 4-5) → Regretful (Ep 6)

### Isolation vs Connection
